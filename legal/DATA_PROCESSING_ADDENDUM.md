# Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

# SCINGOS / SCINGULAR AI/OS Platform

# Inspection Systems Direct LLC

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") supplements the Enterprise Service Agreement and governs the processing of Personal Data.

Effective Date: 2025

---

## 1. Definitions

**Personal Data**: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under applicable Data Protection Laws.

**Data Protection Laws**: All applicable laws and regulations relating to privacy and data protection, including GDPR, CCPA, and other jurisdictional requirements.

**Controller**: The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data (typically the Customer).

**Processor**: The entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller (Provider in this context).

**Sub-processor**: Any third party engaged by Provider to process Personal Data.

---

## 2. Scope and Application

### 2.1 Role of Parties

Customer acts as the Controller, and Provider acts as the Processor with respect to Personal Data processed under the Enterprise Service Agreement.

### 2.2 Processing Activities

Provider will process Personal Data only as necessary to provide the Services and as instructed by Customer through the Platform.

---

## 3. Data Processing Obligations

### 3.1 Processing Instructions

Provider shall process Personal Data only:

- As documented in this DPA and the Agreement
- As instructed by Customer through the Platform
- As required by applicable law

### 3.2 Confidentiality

Provider ensures that persons authorized to process Personal Data:

- Are bound by confidentiality obligations
- Receive appropriate training on data protection

### 3.3 Data Minimization

Provider will process only the Personal Data necessary to provide the Services.

---

## 4. Security Measures

### 4.1 Technical and Organizational Measures

Provider implements appropriate security measures, including:

- **Encryption**: Data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3+) and at rest (AES-256)
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access control (RBAC) and multi-factor authentication
- **Audit Logging**: Immutable audit trails via BANE security framework
- **Network Security**: Firewall protection, intrusion detection, and DDoS mitigation
- **Vulnerability Management**: Regular security assessments and patching
- **Incident Response**: Documented procedures for security incidents

### 4.2 Security Reviews

Provider conducts annual third-party security audits and maintains SOC 2 Type II compliance.

## 6. Data Subject Rights

### 6.1 Assistance

Provider will assist Customer in responding to Data Subject requests, including:

- Access requests
- Rectification requests
- Deletion requests ("right to be forgotten")
- Data portability requests
- Objection to processing

### 6.2 Tools and Interfaces

Provider provides tools within the Platform to enable Customer to fulfill Data Subject requests.

---

## 7. Data Breach Notification

### 7.1 Notification Obligation

Provider will notify Customer without undue delay upon becoming aware of a Personal Data breach, and in any case within 72 hours.

### 7.2 Breach Information

Notification will include:

- Nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of affected Data Subjects
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

### 7.3 Cooperation

Provider will cooperate with Customer's breach response and regulatory notification obligations.

## 9. Data Retention and Deletion

### 9.1 Retention Period

Provider retains Personal Data only for as long as necessary to provide the Services or as required by law.

### 9.2 Deletion Upon Termination

Upon termination of the Agreement, Provider will:

- Delete or return all Personal Data within 30 days (or as specified)
- Certify deletion in writing upon request
- Retain only data required by law

### 9.3 Backup Data

Backup copies will be deleted in accordance with Provider's backup retention policies, typically within 90 days.

---

## 10. Audit Rights

### 10.1 Customer Audit Rights

Customer may audit Provider's compliance with this DPA:

- Upon reasonable notice (minimum 30 days)
- No more than once per year, unless required by law or following a breach
- At Customer's expense
- Subject to confidentiality obligations

### 10.2 Audit Reports

Provider may provide audit reports (SOC 2, ISO 27001) in lieu of on-site audits.

---

## 11. Liability and Indemnification

### 11.1 Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations set forth in the main Agreement.

### 11.2 Regulatory Fines

If Provider's breach of this DPA results in regulatory fines imposed on Customer, Provider will reimburse reasonable fines and penalties, subject to the liability cap.

---

## 12. Term and Termination

### 12.1 Term

This DPA remains in effect for the duration of the Agreement.

### 12.2 Survival

Provisions relating to data deletion, confidentiality, and liability survive termination.

---

## 13. Specific Regional Requirements

### 13.1 GDPR (European Union)

For processing subject to GDPR:

- Provider acts as a Processor under Article 28 GDPR
- Standard Contractual Clauses apply to transfers outside the EEA
- Data Subject rights are honored per Chapter III GDPR

### 13.2 CCPA (California)

For processing subject to CCPA:

- Provider qualifies as a Service Provider
- Provider will not sell Personal Information
- Provider will not retain, use, or disclose Personal Information except as necessary to provide the Services

### 13.3 Other Jurisdictions

Provider will comply with applicable data protection laws in other jurisdictions as reasonably necessary.
