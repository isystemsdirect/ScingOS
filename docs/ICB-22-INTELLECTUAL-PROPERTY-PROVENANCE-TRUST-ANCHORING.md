## 1. PURPOSE

ICB-22 defines how SCINGULAR:

- Protects intellectual property
- Preserves provenance of data and logic
- Anchors trust in system outputs
- Defends against misuse or misrepresentation
- Supports legal and commercial defensibility

This block establishes trust in the platform itself.

---

## 2. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY CLASSES

SCINGULAR SHALL classify IP as:

- Core platform logic
- Inspection codex (ICBs)
- Automation and decision-support models
- Data schemas and formats
- Reports and derived outputs

Ownership SHALL be declared and recorded.

---

## 3. PROVENANCE TRACKING

SCINGULAR SHALL track provenance for:

- Inspection logic execution
- Evidence creation
- Report generation
- Automated recommendations
- Amendments and overrides

Provenance SHALL include:

- Source
- Time
- Actor (human or system)
- Version identifiers

---

## 4. TRUST ANCHORS

SCINGULAR SHALL implement trust anchors including:

- Immutable identifiers
- Cryptographic hashes or signatures
- Version binding
- Authority attribution

Trust anchors SHALL enable verification of authenticity.

---

## 5. MISUSE & MISREPRESENTATION PREVENTION

SCINGULAR SHALL:

- Prevent alteration of official outputs
- Detect unauthorized copying or reuse
- Flag outputs used outside authorized context
- Restrict branding and attribution misuse

---

## 6. REPORT & OUTPUT ATTRIBUTION

All outputs SHALL:

- Identify originating system
- Identify governing authority
- Identify applicable codex versions
- State scope and limitations

Removal of attribution invalidates trust.

---

## 7. LICENSING & USAGE CONTROLS

SCINGULAR SHALL support:

- License enforcement
- Usage scope restrictions
- Tenant-specific rights
- Revocation when violated

License violations SHALL be logged.

---

## 8. THIRD-PARTY CONTENT & DEPENDENCIES

When third-party content is used:

- Source SHALL be identified
- License terms SHALL be respected
- Usage SHALL be scoped and tracked


END ICB-22
