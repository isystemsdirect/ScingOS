## 1. PURPOSE

ICB-03 defines the legal boundaries governing:

- Inspection authority
- Inspector scope
- Liability containment
- Report defensibility
- System risk controls

**THIS BLOCK PREVENTS UNAUTHORIZED OR LEGALLY UNSOUND ACTIONS.**

---

## 2. SOURCES OF LAW (LEGAL CLASSES)

SCINGULAR SHALL recognize four legal classes:

### 2.1 STATUTORY LAW

- Enacted legislation
- Adopted codes
- Environmental statutes
- Occupational safety acts
- Housing and habitability laws

### 2.2 ADMINISTRATIVE LAW

- Agency regulations
- Enforcement procedures
- Licensing rules
- Inspection protocols

### 2.3 CASE LAW

- Judicial interpretations
- Liability precedents
- Scope-of-duty rulings
- Negligence standards

### 2.4 PERMIT & CONTRACT LAW

- Permit conditions
- Consent decrees
- Site-specific obligations

---

## 3. INSPECTOR AUTHORITY LIMITS

Inspectors MAY:

- Observe and document visible conditions
- Measure and test within authorized scope
- Cite violations
- Require corrective actions
- Issue stop-work orders when empowered

Inspectors MAY NOT:

- Perform destructive testing without authorization
- Approve alternative designs
- Provide engineering or architectural certification
- Guarantee future performance
- Assume owner or contractor responsibility

---

## 4. LIABILITY ALLOCATION PRINCIPLES

**INSPECTION DOES NOT TRANSFER LIABILITY.**

Primary liability remains with:

- Owner
- Operator
- Contractor
- Responsible party under law

Inspector liability is limited to:

- Failure to identify visible hazards
- Actions outside authorized scope
- Misrepresentation or fraud

---

## 5. NEGLIGENCE & STANDARD OF CARE

SCINGULAR SHALL assume:

- Inspections are reasonable, not exhaustive
- Concealed conditions are excluded
- Conditions change after inspection

Reports SHALL:

- State scope limitations
- Identify uninspected areas
- Disclaim non-observable conditions

---

## 6. REPORT LEGAL DEFENSIBILITY RULES

All reports MUST:

- Cite applicable authority
- Separate facts from opinion
- Avoid design advice
- Use objective, neutral language
- Preserve original issuance version

Amendments SHALL:

- Be logged
- Be time-stamped
- Preserve original content

---

## 7. EVIDENCE & DISCOVERY READINESS

All records SHALL be treated as:

- Potentially discoverable
- Subject to subpoena
- Subject to audit

Evidence MUST be:

- Tamper-evident
- Time-stamped
- Inspector-attributable
- Retained per law

---

## 8. DISCLAIMERS & SYSTEM SAFEGUARDS

SCINGULAR SHALL enforce:

- Automatic jurisdictional disclaimers
- Scope limitation statements
- Credential-to-scope matching
- Boundary violation warnings

---

## 9. FAILURE MODES & ESCALATION

If legal or scope ambiguity exists:

- Inspection SHALL pause
- Escalation SHALL occur
- User override SHALL be blocked

---

## 10. INHERITANCE RULE

**ICB-03 OVERRIDES ALL SUBSEQUENT ICBs ON MATTERS OF LEGAL CONSTRAINT.**
