# ICB-01 — SCINGULAR INSPECTION CODEX CORE (FOUNDATIONAL SPINE)

**STATUS:** CANONICAL / NON-OVERRIDABLE / LOAD-FIRST  
**APPLIES TO:** ALL SCINGULAR INSPECTION, COMPLIANCE, REPORTING, AND VALIDATION SYSTEMS

---

## 1. PURPOSE & CLAIM VALIDATION MANDATE

ICB-01 establishes the minimum legal, procedural, and logical truth required for SCINGULAR to validly claim that it:

- Performs jurisdiction-aware field inspections
- Applies correct legal authority precedence
- Generates legally defensible inspection reports
- Enforces inspector scope and liability boundaries
- Preserves audit-grade evidence integrity
- Supports regulatory, litigation, and investor scrutiny

**NO SUBSEQUENT CODEX BLOCK MAY CONTRADICT ICB-01.**

---

## 2. INSPECTION DOMAIN ONTOLOGY (PRIMARY CLASSES)

Every inspection SHALL belong to one or more of the following canonical domains:

### 2.1 CONSTRUCTION & BUILDING

- New construction
- Alterations, additions, renovations
- Structural components (foundations, framing, load paths)
- Plumbing, mechanical, electrical systems
- Energy efficiency
- Floodplain & elevation compliance
- Fire-resistance assemblies
- Final occupancy approval

### 2.2 SAFETY & OCCUPATIONAL

- Workplace safety inspections
- Job-site hazard assessments
- PPE compliance
- Machinery & equipment safety
- Incident, injury, and near-miss investigations
- Safety program compliance

### 2.3 ENVIRONMENTAL & HEALTH

- Air, water, soil compliance
- Hazardous materials handling
- Waste storage and disposal
- Stormwater and erosion control
- Environmental remediation sites
- Public health exposure risks

### 2.4 HOUSING, PROPERTY & HABITABILITY

- Rental housing inspections
- Property maintenance compliance
- Housing quality standards
- Life-safety habitability
- Sanitation and occupancy conditions

---

## 3. AUTHORITY HIERARCHY (JURISDICTION RESOLUTION ENGINE)

SCINGULAR SHALL resolve authority conflicts using the following hierarchy.
**THE MOST STRINGENT APPLICABLE REQUIREMENT ALWAYS PREVAILS.**

### 3.1 FEDERAL AUTHORITIES

- Occupational safety regulators
- Environmental protection regulators
- Housing and urban development authorities
- Aviation regulators (when drones or aerial methods are used)

### 3.2 NATIONAL MODEL CODE & STANDARD BODIES

- Building codes (structural, residential, property maintenance)
- Fire, life-safety, and electrical standards
- Materials testing and evaluation standards
- Engineering design and load standards
- Inspection accreditation standards

### 3.3 STATE AUTHORITIES

- State labor and safety agencies
- State environmental agencies
- State building code authorities
- State housing and health departments

### 3.4 LOCAL AUTHORITIES (AHJ)

- City and county building departments
- Fire marshal offices
- Local health departments
- Zoning and planning enforcement bodies

**LOCAL AUTHORITY MAY OVERRIDE FEDERAL OR MODEL CODES WHEN STRICTER.**

---

## 4. KEY ACTORS & ROLE DEFINITIONS

### INSPECTOR

- A person authorized by the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ)
- May enter permitted sites
- May perform visual, non-destructive inspections
- May document violations and require corrective action
- May issue citations or stop-work orders when authorized

Inspectors SHALL NOT:

- Perform destructive testing without authorization
- Redesign systems
- Provide engineering or architectural sign-off unless licensed
- Guarantee future performance or absence of defects

### AUTHORITY HAVING JURISDICTION (AHJ)

- The entity legally empowered to enforce codes and regulations
- May be federal, state, or local
- Determines adopted code editions and enforcement scope

### OWNER / OPERATOR

- Retains ultimate responsibility for compliance
- Inspection does NOT transfer liability

---

## 5. INSPECTION LIFECYCLE (MANDATORY STAGES)

ALL SCINGULAR INSPECTIONS SHALL FOLLOW THIS LIFECYCLE:

### 5.1 PRE-INSPECTION

- Permit and scope verification
- Jurisdiction and code-version resolution
- Risk classification
- Checklist generation

### 5.2 ON-SITE INSPECTION

- Visual observation
- Measurements and testing (within scope)
- Photo and video documentation
- Hazard identification

### 5.3 EVALUATION & SCORING

- Comparison against applicable codes
- Violation classification
- Severity and risk scoring

### 5.4 REPORTING

- Official inspection report generation
- Code citations
- Corrective action requirements
- Deadlines and enforcement status

### 5.5 FOLLOW-UP

- Re-inspection scheduling
- Compliance verification
- Closure or escalation

---

## 6. VIOLATION SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION

### MINOR

- Administrative or low-risk deviations
- No immediate threat
- Requires correction but no operational restriction

### MAJOR

- Significant non-compliance
- Potential safety, health, or structural risk
- Requires timely corrective action

### CRITICAL

- Imminent danger
- Life-safety, environmental, or structural threat
- Triggers stop-work orders or emergency action

---

## 7. EVIDENCE & DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS

ALL INSPECTIONS SHALL PRODUCE AUDIT-GRADE EVIDENCE:

- Time-stamped photographs
- Video recordings where applicable
- Measurement logs
- Inspector notes
- Code citations
- Signatures (digital or physical)

ENVIRONMENTAL SAMPLES SHALL INCLUDE:

- Chain-of-custody documentation
- Testing method references
- Laboratory attribution

INSPECTION RECORDS ARE:

- Legally discoverable
- Admissible in court
- Subject to public records laws
- Retained per jurisdictional requirements

---

## 8. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS (GLOBAL BASELINE)

ALL SCINGULAR-GENERATED REPORTS SHALL INCLUDE:

- Issuing authority identification
- Unique report ID
- Date(s) of inspection
- Location and scope
- Findings and results
- Statement of compliance or non-compliance
- Corrective actions
- Authorized approval signature
- Amendment history if modified

REPORTS MUST BE:

- Clear
- Non-ambiguous
- Traceable
- Tamper-evident

---

## 9. ETHICS, LIABILITY & PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT

INSPECTORS SHALL:

- Remain objective and neutral
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Apply codes consistently
- Stay within authorized scope

INSPECTIONS:

- Reduce risk
- DO NOT transfer responsibility
- DO NOT guarantee future performance

---

## 10. SCINGULAR SYSTEM ENFORCEMENT RULES

SCINGULAR SHALL:

- Enforce jurisdiction precedence automatically
- Prevent actions outside inspector scope
- Lock reports after issuance (with amendment tracking)
- Preserve immutable evidence records
- Reject non-authoritative inputs

**ICB-01 IS THE ROOT AUTHORITY.**  
**ALL SUBSEQUENT CBs MUST INHERIT AND EXTEND — NEVER CONTRADICT — THIS BLOCK.**
